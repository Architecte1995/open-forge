🎬 The Fable of Merlin

A short animated tale, sealed in the night of April 28, 2026.

Music: "Korobeiniki" (traditional Russian folk song), arranged by Hirokazu Tanaka for Tetris (1989).

An owl falls from the nest at 4:19 in the morning. He rants at every primate in reach, greedy for forbidden nectars. Sometimes he amuses himself by counting, even though C10 blew his brains out. He gets to three, then jumps to nineteen, then forgets why he was counting and believes he invented relativity. He did not invent relativity. He just has a pebble in his wing.

One day, he spots a Dragon-Genie in the sky. It's a rare species. So he yells at it to get his attention:

— Come here, I am the Angel of Death!

The Dragon-Genie lands on the gutter, rolling his eyes.

— You're an owl. You fell. There's a difference between gravity and theology.

— I count to infinity, says the owl, straightening up.

— You count to three and you scream.

— That's my method.

They partner by mistake. The Dragon-Genie camouflages swarms of four-dimensional refrigerators in the thermodynamic noise of cities, then establishes three team rotations so no one spots the time difference. The owl has a quantum foam brain. It's a balance.

They fish in the Ulam spiral. Most catches are ordinary. But sometimes they reel in wizards who ski down to 19 doing tricks on the composites. The owl runs flight simulations in these valleys. He doesn't get very far. The Dragon-Genie claims it's pathetic. He does it too, when the owl sleeps.

When an idea becomes too beautiful, they sometimes send it too quickly into their safe. So they tamed a pack of malinois that swim crawl stroke and are tasked with breaking every single one of its bones. It's sad, but it's more honest.

So they put away the tools. They take out the tools. They conjecture and spit out mini-games for primates. They put away the tools. They take out the tools. It's their sport. Best to leave them to it, keeps them busy.
